â… . Game Overview

Game Type

Strategy Card Game, Resource Management

Number of Players

2-4

Game Duration

45-60 minutes

Game Objective

Accumulate the most ATP and complete anabolic objectives by building an efficient metabolic network within a specified number of turns.

â…¡. Educational Objectives

This game aims to help players understand through an interactive board game format:

  1. The interconnections among carbohydrate, lipid, amino acid, and nucleotide metabolism.
  2. The central role of enzymes in metabolic regulation.
  3. Hormonal and energetic state regulation of metabolism.
  4. Metabolic division of labor among different cell types.
  5. Metabolic disorders under pathological conditions.

â…£. Game Setup

  1. Preparation:
    • Each player receives:
      • 1 Hepatocyte card (placed in front)
      • 3 Glucose cards
      • 5 ATP tokens
      • 2 random Amino Acid cards
    • Shuffle all decks (Metabolite, Enzyme, Hormone, State, Objective)
    • Place decks in the common area
    • Determine the starting player (player with the least ATP goes first)
  2. Game Board
    • Central board displays a metabolic pathway map
    • Four zones: Cell Zone, Metabolic Zone, Enzyme Activation Zone, State Zone

â…¤. Game Turn Sequence

Each turn proceeds through the following phases:

Phase 1: State Update (Simultaneous)
  • Check and apply State card effects
  • Remove expired Enzyme/Hormone effects
  • Pay maintenance costs (1 ATP per cell)
Phase 2: Draw Phase
  • Draw 3 cards from the Metabolite deck
  • Brain Cell extra ability: May spend 1 Action Point to draw 1 Skill card (Enzyme/Hormone/State)
Phase 3: Action Phase (Core Phase)

Each player has a base of 3 Action Points, plus +1 Action Point for each owned Myocyte. Players need to follow the Available Actions below: (Each costs 1 Action Point)

B. Cell Proliferation and Differentiation

  • Synthesizing a new cell requires fulfilling its metabolite requirements and spending corresponding Action Points
  • Place the new Cell card in front of you, immediately gaining its special ability

C. Enzyme and Hormone Activation

  • When using an Enzyme card, place in Activation Zone, effect lasts for its duration
  • Pancreatic Islet Cell spends 2 Amino Acids to synthesize Insulin/Glucagon
  • Effect triggers immediately, lasts 3 turns

D. Special Actions

1. Cori Cycle (Hepatocyte + Myocyte cooperation is needed)
Transport 1 Lactate from Myocyte to Hepatocyte
Hepatocyte spends 2 ATP to convert it into 1 Glucose
2. Storage and Retrieval
Store metabolites in Adipocyte (unlimited capacity)
Retrieve stored substances (costs Action Points)
Phase 4: Cleanup Phase
  1. Discard unused Glucose cards (for its inability to store)
  2. Hand limit is 10 cards, discard any excess
  3. Remove expired State cards
Phase 5: Check Synthesis Objectives
  • Check if requirements of Synthesis Objective cards are met
  • Upon completion, claim ATP reward, may draw a new objective

â…¥. Metabolic Regulation Mechanisms

1. Feedback Regulation of Energy State

  • High-Energy State (ATP>15): Inhibits catabolism, promotes anabolism
  • Low-Energy State (ATP<5): Promotes catabolism, inhibits anabolism
  • Manifested as reduced costs/increased yields for certain reactions under low-energy state

2. Hormonal Hierarchy Regulation

High Insulin levels â†’ Activates Glycolysis, Glycogen synthesis, Fat synthesis enzymes

High Glucagon levels â†’ Activates Glycogenolysis, Gluconeogenesis, Fat breakdown enzymes

Adrenaline â†’ Emergency state, prioritizes Glycogenolysis activation

Cortisol â†’ Long-term stress, activates Gluconeogenesis and protein breakdown
